Concurrency

Concurrency in iOS means executing multiple tasks at the same time — or at least appearing to — to keep the UI responsive and make full use of multi-core hardware. iOS provides a layered set of concurrency tools: from the low-level Grand Central Dispatch (GCD) C API, to the object-oriented Operation/OperationQueue layer, to the modern Swift async/await and structured concurrency model introduced in Swift 5.5 / iOS 15. Concurrency is one of the most tested interview topics because it sits at the intersection of correctness (race conditions, deadlocks) and performance (thread utilisation, responsiveness). This section covers every layer of the stack and the tradeoffs between them.
